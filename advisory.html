Mancozeb 75% WP at 2.5g/litre at first sign of leaf blight. Spray 2–3 times at weekly intervals.

Harvest Stage

Day 95 – Day 110 · Reap what you sowed

Harvest Readiness Checks

🌽
Husk & Kernel Check
Peel back husk — kernels should be firm, fully dented, and show a black layer at the base (black layer test).
💧
Moisture Check
Target grain moisture: 20–25% for fresh market, 12–14% for storage. Use a moisture meter.
📸
Final Harvest Photo
Submit a harvest photo with harvested cobs clearly visible. Required to close your Suraksha record.

Post-Harvest Tips

🌡️ Dry grain to 12–14% moisture before storing to prevent mould and aflatoxin formation.

Pest & Disease Library

Know Your Enemies

🐛
Fall Armyworm
● High Risk
Feeds on whorl and leaves of young maize plants. Droppings visible in whorl. Severe infestation causes "dead heart" in seedlings.
Treatment: Coragen 0.4ml/L · Day 20–25
🦗
Stem Borer
● Medium Risk
Bores into the stem, causing characteristic "window pane" feeding marks on leaves. Weakens plant structure leading to lodging.
Treatment: Chlorpyrifos 2ml/L · Day 30–35
🌿
Weeds (Mixed)
● High Risk — Early Season
Competitive weeds in early season can reduce yield by 30–50%. Grass and broadleaf weeds compete for nutrients and moisture.
Treatment: Atrazine 1kg/acre · Day 3–5
🍂
Maize Leaf Blight
● Medium Risk
Fungal disease causing large tan-brown lesions on leaves. Spreads in wet humid conditions. Reduces photosynthesis and yield.
Treatment: Mancozeb 2.5g/L · Day 50–55
🟡
Maize Streak Virus
● Low Risk
Yellow streaking on leaves caused by leafhopper-transmitted virus. Use virus-tolerant hybrid varieties and control leafhopper vectors.
Control: Use tolerant varieties like DKC 9108
🦟
Aphids
● Low Risk — Monitor
Small sap-sucking insects on undersides of leaves. Heavy infestation causes leaf curl and honeydew deposits. Often controlled by natural predators.
Treatment: Thiamethoxam 0.25g/L if severe
Best Practices

General Farming Tips

⏰
Spray Timing
Always spray pesticides early morning (6–9 AM) or late evening. Avoids heat evaporation and bee harm.
💧
Water Quality
Use clean water for mixing. Hard water reduces effectiveness of some insecticides. Add a spreader-sticker for better coverage.
🔄
Rotate Chemicals
Rotate pesticide classes each season to prevent resistance development in pests like Fall Armyworm.
🧤
Safety First
Always wear gloves, mask, and eyewear while mixing and spraying. Wash hands thoroughly after handling any chemical.
📋
Read the Label
The product label is a legal document. Always follow label instructions for dosage, timing, and pre-harvest intervals.
🌡️
Temperature Check
Don't spray when temperatures exceed 35°C — chemicals degrade faster and may damage crops (phytotoxicity).
📷
Document Everything
Photo your crop at every stage. It supports your Suraksha record and helps our experts give you better advice.
📞
Ask When Unsure
Never guess. WhatsApp us a crop photo and our agronomist will respond with specific advice within 2 hours.
